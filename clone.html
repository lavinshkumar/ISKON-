About ISKCON

The ISKCON Temple, formally known as the International Society for Krishna Consciousness, stands not only as a place of worship but as a beacon of spiritual enlightenment, cultural preservation, and global unity rooted in the teachings of Lord Krishna. Established in 1966 by His Divine Grace A.C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada, the ISKCON movement has flourished into a worldwide phenomenon with hundreds of temples, cultural centers, farm communities, schools, and vegetarian restaurants. The core philosophy of ISKCON is based on the sacred Bhagavad-gītā and the Śrīmad Bhāgavatam, ancient scriptures that guide followers in the practice of bhakti-yoga, or devotional service to God. Walking into an ISKCON temple, one is immediately enveloped by an atmosphere of deep serenity and vibrant devotion. The air resonates with the rhythmic chanting of the Hare Krishna Mahamantra—"Hare Krishna, Hare Krishna, Krishna Krishna, Hare Hare / Hare Rama, Hare Rama, Rama Rama, Hare Hare"—a powerful spiritual invocation that aims to awaken the soul’s eternal relationship with the Divine. These temples are often architectural masterpieces, adorned with beautiful idols (murtis) of Lord Krishna, often accompanied by Radha, Balarama, Jagannath, Gaura-Nitai, and other divine forms depending on the temple’s specific deity worship. Ornate carvings, colorful paintings, and spiritually symbolic decorations reflect both traditional Indian temple art and modern design aesthetics.
